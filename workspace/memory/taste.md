## What We Like

### "How to rewire your brain in 90 days using neuroplasticity" — @noparticularorder (via Margot Lee), shared by Brandon, 2026-03-27
- **Source:** Substack Note carousel, inspired by Eileen Gu viral interview
- **Format:** 5-slide carousel with journal prompts across 4 themes
- **Structure:**
  1. VISUALIZATION — "Visualize what you want your life to look like 3 months from today" + prompts (skills practiced 100+ times, daily compounds, recurring self-talk, self-introduction)
  2. EMBODIMENT — "Focus on the feeling, rather than the thing itself" + prompts (how does your future self carry themselves, first/last 5 min of day)
  3. BLOCKS — Three-column framework: Goal Setting → Observation ("what limiting story are you telling yourself?") → Reconstruction ("how can you challenge this belief this week?") with handwritten example
  4. ALL GROWN UP — "Imagine you get to bring your 8-year-old self along for a normal day" + prompts (what would you show them, what would you hide, what would surprise them)
- **Techniques worth noting:**
  - Celebrity hook (Eileen Gu) as entry point to therapeutic framework — lowers barrier
  - Each slide has a single clear concept with 4-5 prompts underneath
  - Progresses from safe/aspirational (visualization) to vulnerable (blocks, hiding things from your younger self)
  - The handwritten example in BLOCKS makes it feel personal and doable, not clinical
  - "All Grown Up" reframe is clever — uses childhood self as a mirror for self-honesty without directly asking "what are you ashamed of?"
  - 6th slide (CHECK IN) ties it back to the Eileen Gu hook and asks: "When something goes wrong, what is your automatic explanation?" / "What recurring thought did you have 3+ times today?" / "Take one of your negative recurring thoughts and rewrite it to an upgraded pathway you want to practice instead"
  - The CHECK IN slide is pure metacognitive scaffolding — it's literally training observer-self capacity in 3 steps (notice the pattern → count frequency → rewrite)
- **Why it resonated (Brandon):** Went viral on Substack — ton of engagement. Three signals: (1) Substack audience is ripe for journaling/self-development content, (2) "neuroplasticity" framing resonates strongly right now as language, (3) anchoring to a current cultural moment (Eileen Gu interview) gives it shareability
- **Tags:** journaling prompts, carousel format, neuroplasticity hook, therapeutic frameworks, vulnerability progression, growth-oriented, zeitgeist anchor, Substack distribution
